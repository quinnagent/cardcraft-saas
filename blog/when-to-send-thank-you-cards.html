When to Send Wedding Thank You Cards: Complete Timeline & Etiquette Guide

One of the most common questions after the wedding is: When do I need to send thank you cards? The good news is you have more time than you think. Here's everything you need to know about timing, etiquette, and getting those cards out without the stress.

The Golden Rule: 3 Months

Traditional Timeline

Etiquette experts recommend sending wedding thank you cards within 3 months of your wedding date. This gives you enough time to:

  • Enjoy your honeymoon
  • Settle into married life
  • Gather all your gift information
  • Write thoughtful, personalized messages

Can You Send Them Later?

Yes! While 3 months is the standard, it's truly never too late to send a thank you card. A late thank you is always better than no thank you at all. If you're past the 3-month mark, simply acknowledge the delay in your message:

"We're sorry this is reaching you later than intended, but please know how much we appreciated having you at our wedding..."

Special Situations

Gifts Received Before the Wedding

Send these thank you cards within 2 weeks of receiving the gift. This shows appreciation and confirms you received it safely.

Gifts Received After the Wedding

Follow the standard 3-month timeline from your wedding date, regardless of when the gift arrived.

Destination Weddings

Guests who traveled especially far? Consider sending these thank you cards first, within 4-6 weeks, while the experience is fresh.
